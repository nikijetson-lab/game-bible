# Quest Brief: The Ferry Oath

## Purpose

`The Ferry Oath` is the second full follow-up quest after the default-canon Local Bargain outcome of `The Hunger Below`.

After `Salt in the Ledger` showed that compromise networks attract parasites, this quest shifts to a structural question: who gets to control the routes that survival depends on?

Where `Salt in the Ledger` was about trust as currency, `The Ferry Oath` is about authority and monopoly over movement itself.

This quest must demonstrate:

- local power consolidation as both solution and risk
- irreconcilable visions of freedom versus stability
- the player being forced to decide who should speak for a place
- the faction system tightening around a single choke point

## Starting State

By this point the player has:

- resolved `The Hunger Below` through Local Bargain
- confronted predatory abuse in the medicine network in `Salt in the Ledger`
- weakened their standing with the administration
- deepened relationships with Мурі, ferrymen, and survival smugglers
- become a known quantity whom local actors now try to recruit, trap, or use

The region is somewhat stabler than at the start, but the informal systems that saved it are now hardening into structures of their own.

## Quest Hook

Tovan Reed proposes that the fen's ferry crossings, moorage rights, and transit taxes be placed under a single clan council with formal route protection. He frames it as protection against both city seizure and smuggler violence.

Nera Vale opposes him publicly, arguing that central control will turn the ferrymen into gatekeepers with too much power over who eats, who travels, and who dies waiting for passage.

The blow-up happens at the main Сонк-Феррі landing in front of traders, patients, and quota observers.

The player is caught as the only neutral person with enough credibility to be heard by both sides.

## Core Mystery

The surface conflict is about governance. The real question is harder: who is right?

The layered truth:

- Tovan Reed genuinely wants stability and route security, but his proposed council would be dominated by his own clan
- Nera Vale fears that concentrated ferry power would lock out vulnerable families, but she has no institutional alternative to offer
- someone has been sabotaging night ferries and destroying mooring lines, creating the crisis that Tovan is exploiting to push his plan
- Brin Oss or Hara Pike's nodes may be involved in the sabotage—either to destabilize toward more smuggling opportunity, or to force a crackdown that benefits a rival
- the administration is watching closely, ready to use the dispute as justification for seizing the ferry network entirely

This makes the immediate mystery (who sabotaged the ferries) inseparable from the larger political question (who should control them).

## Main Cast

### Tovan Reed

Clan spokesman.

Role:
Wants to unify the fen's water routes under a clan council he would effectively lead.

Use:
Represents order-without-the-city—pragmatic, locally legitimate, but ambitious and consolidating power under the cover of stability.

His case is not dishonest, but it is designed to benefit him.

### Nera Vale

Ferrymaster, first witness from `The Hunger Below`.

Role:
Wants local route control to stay diffuse, responsive, and independently held.

Use:
Represents freedom through fragmentation—authentically concerned about abuse of power, but unable to offer a structural alternative that prevents chaos.

Her fear is real but her position is reactive.

### The Silent Party

A saboteur whose identity the player must uncover.

Identity options (designed to react to the player's previous choices):

- a smuggler faction operative trying to destabilize toward open black-market control
- a quota hardliner trying to create a crisis that justifies administrative takeover
- a ferryman acting out of personal grievance and using the political moment as cover
- a clan isolationist who wants all crossings destroyed rather than formalized

### Marshal-Recorder Serit Kelm (background pressure)

City legal officer.

Role:
Appears in the margins, observing, taking notes, waiting for the dispute to escalate enough to justify intervention.

Use:
Embodies the cost of losing local control—the threat that if locals cannot govern themselves, the city will do it for them.

### Brin Oss (potential witness)

Logistics broker.

Role:
Knows who moves in the dark water routes and may offer information at a price that binds the player further into the smuggling economy.

## Quest Structure

### Phase 1: Open Rupture

The player arrives at the Сонк-Феррі landing after the public clash.

Opening conditions:

- Tovan Reed presents a written proposal for the route council
- Nera Vale refuses and calls on the ferrymen to hold independent
- angry traders demand immediate arbitration
- a city observer is noting names and faction alignments
- the body of a drowned ferryman was found the night before with cut mooring rope around his wrist

Goal:
Establish that this is not a simple argument—it is a crisis with a corpse attached.

### Phase 2: Tracing the Sabotage

The player investigates who damaged the ferries and cut the ropes.

Investigation lanes:

- examine the bodies and damaged moorings for tool marks, fiber traces, and boat type
- question ferrymen about who was active on the water on the relevant nights
- trace route logs and fuel or cargo discrepancies
- follow the economic chain: who benefits directly from route disruption
- interview those who have already chosen sides to find if the split preceded the sabotage

What this phase should reveal:

- more than one actor has been working the water at night
- the sabotage is methodical, not panicked
- the sabotage directly benefits Tovan's narrative, but Tovan is competent enough to be innocent or controlled enough to be guilty
- some ferrymen are being paid not to talk by a source they will not name

### Phase 3: The Political Fork

The player must decide how to handle the public dispute while the sabotage investigation continues.

Key decision points:

- mediate a temporary truce (buys time, but convinces no one)
- side openly with Tovan's council proposal (risk of accelerating local authoritarian drift)
- side openly with Nera's diffuse control (risk of no actual governance)
- propose an entirely different structure (requires leverage, allies, and legitimacy)
- escalate or suppress the sabotage findings depending on political usefulness

Design note:
This phase is where the quest proves it understands that investigation and politics are the same activity, not separate phases.

### Phase 4: Preparation

Preparation here is about building coalition, leverage, or enforcement capacity.

Available actions:

- recruit ferrymen loyal to Nera to patrol and protect remaining crossings
- gather testimony from witnesses who feared Tovan's consolidation or from those who fear chaos more
- secure a written commitment from Brin Oss or other smuggling actors to stay neutral or reveal the saboteur
- create a documented position on ferry rights that can be presented to Serit Kelm as local governance evidence
- identify which clan alliances Tovan is relying on and whether any can be split

### Phase 5: Confrontation—The Night Council

The quest culminates at a night meeting on a damaged ferry dock, attended by ferrymen, clan heads, traders, shrine observers, and smuggler representatives.

The player must:

- present findings on the sabotage
- state a position on route governance
- survive the confrontation being tested by every party present

The confrontation includes:

- verbal testimony and cross-accusation
- a dramatic reveal of the saboteur (or concealment)
- visible factions threatening to walk or escalate
- the saboteur may attempt violence, flight, or a counter-accusation
- Serit Kelm's observer taking notes that may become legal foundation

## Doctrine-Specific Strengths

### Judicator

Strong options:

- conduct the Night Council as a formal proceeding
- present evidence of sabotage as fact-finding, not faction warfare
- impose a binding temporary structure with force of testimony

Risk:
The formal frame may fail if the parties do not recognize the player's procedural authority.

### Stalker

Strong options:

- reconstruct the saboteur's movements with field-level accuracy
- find physical evidence others overlooked
- turn the investigation into a definitive chase

Risk:
Solving the crime may not resolve the political crisis.

### Lantern

Strong options:

- read the hidden fears that drive both Tovan and Nera
- interpret the sabotage as a symptom of spiritual strain on the water
- use oath and rite language to bind a public agreement

Risk:
Spiritual authority may not hold when grain and profit are at stake.

### Broker

Strong options:

- negotiate separate deals with both sides simultaneously
- trade the saboteur's identity for concessions on route governance
- fragment Tovan's clan coalition through targeted offers or threats

Risk:
Every deal deepens the player's entanglement in local logrolling.

## Resolution Paths

### Outcome A: Tovan's Council Prevails

The route council is formed under clan leadership with the player's support, explicit or implicit.

Effects:

- route security improves measurably
- ferry governance solidifies into a power block
- Nera Vale and independent ferrymen lose influence
- the player gains a powerful local ally with expectations of reciprocity
- the administration has a single entity to pressure or subvert

### Outcome B: Diffuse Control Preserved

The player backs Nera's vision: no single authority, independent crossings, and route decisions kept at family level.

Effects:

- local freedom of movement survives
- no one consolidates enough power to protect the routes systematically
- sabotage becomes harder to trace permanently
- the player gains trust among independent ferrymen and clan dissidents
- the administration dismisses local capacity as inadequate

### Outcome C: Player-Imposed Structure

The player proposes and enforces an alternative: a rotating oversight council, a Warden-backed route charter, or a temporary governance model with built-in review.

Effects:

- neither Tovan nor Nera gets full control
- the player assumes governing responsibility for the routes
- long-term benefit depends on the player's continued presence
- both sides have grievances but are held in a fragile, functional balance
- Serit Kelm takes notes for future administrative argument

### Outcome D: Administrative Seizure

The player fails to resolve the dispute, or allows it to escalate until Serit Kelm intervenes and the city takes formal control of the ferry network.

Effects:

- route governance passes to the administration
- ferrymen become salaried civil employees under city supervision
- smuggling is suppressed initially, then re-emerges under costlier conditions
- the player loses influence with both local camps
- the bitter lesson is learned: if the fen cannot govern its own routes, someone else will

## Reputation Consequences

Public Memory:
This quest will define how the fen sees the player's relationship to local power. Protector of independence, stabilizer, aspiring ruler, or witness to failure.

Faction Standing:
Significant and asymmetrical shifts with Мурі, ferrymen, smugglers, and the administration. Some doors close permanently depending on the choice.

Professional Reputation:
Likely trait reinforcement: dependable, lawful, compromised, or relentless.

## Why This Quest Matters

`The Ferry Oath` is where the game asks whether the player will govern or merely intervene. `The Hunger Below` was about survival. `Salt in the Ledger` was about trusting broken systems. This quest is about whether the player is willing to become the authority they thought they were investigating.

## Follow-Up Hooks

This quest should feed directly into:

- `Ash Under the Chapel`, as the player's visible authority draws more requests and imitations
- `The Quota Knife`, as route control attracts administrative attention
- a long-term relationship with Tovan Reed as ally, rival, or dependent
- questions about the player's identity as Warden versus local power holder
