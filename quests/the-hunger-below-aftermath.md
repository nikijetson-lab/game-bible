# The Hunger Below: Aftermath Matrix

## Purpose

This document defines how the world reacts to each major resolution path of `The Hunger Below`.

The goal is to make consequences legible, systemic, and narratively productive. Each outcome should alter:

- local public mood
- faction standing directions
- professional reputation signals
- follow-up quest pressure
- visible conditions in The Hazemoor

## Consequence Design Rule

No ending should feel like the clean correct answer.

Every ending should:

- solve one immediate pressure
- worsen or expose another pressure
- strengthen some actors while destabilizing others
- create a distinct emotional memory of the player's style

## Outcome A: Public Exposure

The player reveals grain diversion, burial crimes, and the flood-chamber breach openly.

### Public Memory

The Hazemoor reaction:

- many common people respect the Warden for breaking the lie
- fear rises because truth arrives together with visible instability
- Сонк-Феррі becomes louder, angrier, and politically volatile

Mood summary:
The Warden is seen as truthful, dangerous, and impossible to control.

### Faction Shifts

Fortress-City Administration:

- strong negative shift
- stabilizers think the player acted without state discipline
- hardliners treat the player as a destabilizing liability

Shrine Keepers:

- mixed to positive shift
- mercy keepers value exposure of burial wrongs
- strict keepers fear spiritual panic and loss of ritual authority

Мурі and Ferrymen:

- moderate positive shift
- locals appreciate the official lie being broken
- some fear city retaliation will now fall on them

Smuggler Chains:

- mixed shift
- predatory smugglers enjoy disorder
- survival smugglers worry that route crackdowns will follow

Warden Remnants:

- confessor-aligned remnants become more interested in the player
- restorer-aligned remnants see the move as reckless truth without institutional containment

### Professional Reputation Signals

Likely trait movement:

- lawful, if done through evidence and public process
- relentless, if exposure comes through pressure and confrontation
- dangerous, almost always

### Visible World Changes

- public ledgers appear guarded or burned
- ration lines become militarized
- new rumor clusters spread through Сонк-Феррі
- Mirefold receives relief but also visits from hostile officials
- Saint Vey's becomes crowded with the frightened and curious

### Follow-Up Pressure

- Serit Kelm arrives to reassert administrative control
- a witness disappears before formal testimony can be secured
- hardliners push an emergency mandate against local ferrymen and shrine keepers

## Outcome B: Controlled Containment

The player suppresses the scandal, neutralizes the Reedbound, and allows limited reform under pressure.

### Public Memory

The Hazemoor reaction:

- the region feels calmer, but not convinced
- many civilians suspect the Warden fixed something without telling them what it cost
- trust becomes practical rather than warm

Mood summary:
The Warden is seen as effective, cold, and possibly complicit.

### Faction Shifts

Fortress-City Administration:

- moderate to strong positive shift
- stabilizers approve the preservation of order
- hardliners still test whether the player can be controlled long term

Shrine Keepers:

- mixed shift
- some respect the reduced panic
- others resent the burial truth remaining suppressed

Мурі and Ferrymen:

- mild negative to mixed shift
- they see another outsider choosing order over full honesty
- practical operators may still respect the result if routes reopen fast

Smuggler Chains:

- generally negative shift among opportunists
- stability shrinks black-market leverage
- some pragmatic smugglers appreciate predictable pressure instead of chaos

Warden Remnants:

- restorers become more interested in the player
- confessors grow suspicious that the Warden is repeating old institutional sins

### Professional Reputation Signals

Likely trait movement:

- lawful
- dependable
- compromised

### Visible World Changes

- guard patrols become more organized and less openly brutal
- supply notices return with tighter language and fewer public details
- Saint Vey's keeps certain records hidden under stronger watch
- the Sunk Watchroad becomes passable faster than expected

### Follow-Up Pressure

- the player is quietly offered more sealed work by the administration
- Mirefold produces a private accusation that someone was sacrificed to keep peace
- a remnant Warden questions whether containment was justice or cowardice

## Outcome C: Local Bargain

The player works through unofficial deals to redirect food, calm violence, and preserve a livable compromise.

### Public Memory

The Hazemoor reaction:

- locals who benefit call the Warden practical and worth trusting
- outsiders and officials call the Warden dirty or bought
- public understanding of events stays fragmented by design

Mood summary:
The Warden is seen as useful, suspect, and close to the mud.

### Faction Shifts

Fortress-City Administration:

- clear negative shift
- the player is treated as a facilitator of leakage, theft, and local insubordination

Shrine Keepers:

- mixed shift
- mercy keepers may respect that people survived
- strict keepers resent compromise with smugglers and unofficial rites

Мурі and Ferrymen:

- strong positive shift
- local communities value survival over procedural cleanliness

Smuggler Chains:

- strong positive shift with split consequences
- survival smugglers see the player as workable
- predatory smugglers try to absorb or corrupt the relationship

Warden Remnants:

- divided response
- some see this as frontier realism
- others see it as further decay of Warden legitimacy

### Professional Reputation Signals

Likely trait movement:

- dependable, in local circles
- compromised
- dangerous, if the bargain required coercion

### Visible World Changes

- more hidden goods appear in back rooms and ferry caches
- Сонк-Феррі authority weakens in subtle ways before open collapse
- Mirefold stabilizes unevenly but faster than expected
- smugglers become bolder at dusk routes

### Follow-Up Pressure

- Brin Oss offers deeper access at a future moral cost
- Hara Pike tries to turn the player's compromise into permanent dependency
- officials prepare a purge disguised as anti-bandit action

## Outcome D: Ritual Mercy

The player prioritizes the dead, containment through proper burial, and spiritual repair even at immediate material cost.

### Public Memory

The Hazemoor reaction:

- fear spikes first, then settles into uneasy reverence
- many people believe the Warden chose the dead over hungry families
- others believe someone finally treated the land with seriousness

Mood summary:
The Warden is seen as uncanny, severe, and not entirely of ordinary civic life.

### Faction Shifts

Fortress-City Administration:

- strong negative shift
- officials interpret this as failure to prioritize supply stability and civil order

Shrine Keepers:

- strong positive shift, especially among mercy-aligned keepers
- strict keepers approve the restoration of burial order but worry about uncontrolled revelation

Мурі and Ferrymen:

- mixed shift
- the spiritually minded respect the act
- the starving may resent it bitterly

Smuggler Chains:

- mixed to negative shift
- they profit from chaos, but dislike sacred unpredictability and increased local taboo zones

Warden Remnants:

- Lantern-leaning and confessor-aligned remnants become deeply interested in the player
- restorers consider the act morally serious but politically disastrous

### Professional Reputation Signals

Likely trait movement:

- uncanny
- lawful, if done through rite and duty rather than frenzy
- dangerous, in the eyes of institutions

### Visible World Changes

- burial sites are restored or newly marked
- Saint Vey's gains mourners, petitioners, and watchers
- some routes remain unsafe because material recovery was delayed
- Сонк-Феррі grows quieter but harsher under shortage discipline

### Follow-Up Pressure

- Mother Ysra Veyn offers access to deeper burial archives
- famine unrest creates a retaliatory populist movement against shrine influence
- a copycat ritual in another village goes wrong without proper guidance

## Canon Guidance

Preferred default-canon outcome: Outcome C, Local Bargain.

Why Outcome C is the strongest default:

- reinforces the frontier's morally dirty survival logic
- creates strong local allies and enemies quickly
- feeds directly into reputation and factional grayness
- preserves instability without collapsing the region too early
- gives later quests room to punish compromise rather than simple heroism

Outcome B remains the strongest alternate canon for a colder, more institutional arc.
Outcome A is excellent for a high-chaos playthrough or a later region escalation.
Outcome D is excellent for a more mystical or Lantern-forward arc.

## Current Design Use

This aftermath matrix should guide:

- regional state changes after the benchmark quest
- faction standing updates
- rumor and dialogue variations
- follow-up quest generation in The Hazemoor
