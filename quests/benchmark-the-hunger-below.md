# Benchmark Quest: The Hunger Below

## Purpose

This is the first benchmark quest for The Hazemoor. It is designed to prove the core fantasy of the game in one contained case.

The quest must demonstrate:

- arrival into a socially unstable settlement
- investigation with contradictory explanations
- preparation that changes the outcome of confrontation
- at least three doctrine-valid approaches
- a resolution with visible regional consequences
- a reveal that links local suffering to buried Compact violence

## Quest Hook

A grain convoy bound for Тихий Шелест vanished near the Sunk Watchroad. Survivors claim the barges were taken by grave-lights and drowned dead. Quota officials blame smugglers. Villagers from Mirefold whisper that the marsh has begun reclaiming what was fed into it generations ago.

The player is asked to investigate because the missing grain threatens famine conditions and public unrest.

## Entry Point

The quest begins in Тихий Шелест.

The player arrives to find:

- ration lines turning violent
- a ferryman beaten for spreading ghost stories
- a quota officer locking down the grain records
- a contract notice offering payment for proof of smuggling or supernatural attack

## Core Mystery

The apparent mystery is simple: what took the convoy?

The real layered truth:

- part of the grain was diverted deliberately by officials skimming supply before the city counts arrived
- smugglers were involved, but not as the primary cause of disappearance
- the convoy route passed over an old flood-chamber breach connected to buried dead from Compact drainage works
- disturbed remains and contaminated water triggered a hostile marsh emergence that witnesses interpret as haunted attack
- someone in Mirefold has been feeding offerings into a drowned shrine to keep the phenomenon away from their own household

This means the case is simultaneously:

- political corruption
- survival-driven local secrecy
- genuine supernatural danger

## Main Cast

### Quartermaster Halren Voss

Тихий Шелест ration authority.

Role:
A practical, cold official trying to keep order while hiding diverted grain records.

Use:
Represents institutional stability at moral cost and ties directly into the Fortress-City Administration split between stabilizers and extraction hardliners.

### Nera Vale

Ferrymaster and first surviving witness.

Role:
Knows the water routes, saw what happened, and is telling the truth badly because terror has broken the sequence in her mind.

Use:
Early key witness with partial evidence.

### Old Sella of Mirefold

A respected village elder tied to shrine offerings.

Role:
Not malicious, but willing to feed old rites if it keeps children alive for another week.

Use:
Embodies survival corroding moral limits.

### Brother Caros

Keeper from Saint Vey's Lantern House.

Role:
Possesses burial fragments and knows the dead are disturbed, but fears public panic and doctrinal scandal.

Use:
Bridge between lore and institutional concealment, and an early face of the split between strict and mercy-minded shrine keepers.

## Quest Structure

### Phase 1: Arrival and Noise

The player enters Тихий Шелест during ration unrest.

Immediate interactions:

- inspect the contract board and missing convoy notice
- break up or exploit a clash between townsfolk and guards
- speak with Nera Vale, who describes lights moving against the current
- observe Quartermaster Voss suppressing access to inventory ledgers

Goal:
Establish urgency, scarcity, and conflicting narratives.

### Phase 2: Inquiry Across the Fen

The player investigates multiple evidence lanes.

Possible evidence sources:

- convoy ledger discrepancies in Тихий Шелест
- drag marks and broken moorings along the Sunk Watchroad
- shrine offerings and trampled footpaths near Mirefold
- burial lists or forbidden drainage records at Saint Vey's Lantern House
- survivor testimony from a delirious patient who heard the water speaking names

What the player learns:

- some grain was never meant to reach the public stores
- human hands altered the route
- something did rise from below the breached flood structure
- Mirefold villagers have been hiding related signs for days

### Phase 3: Interpretation

The player assembles a working theory.

This is a critical design beat: the quest should not force a single simplistic conclusion too early.

Possible interim beliefs:

- it is mainly smuggling covered with ghost stories
- it is mainly a dead-disturbance triggered by old burial desecration
- it is a mixed case of corruption causing supernatural consequences
- the shrine offerings are containment rather than worship

The final shape of the confrontation and certain dialogue options should react to what the player has inferred and prepared for.

### Phase 4: Preparation

Before the final push, the player can prepare through several channels.

Preparation options:

- acquire marsh-fire warding compounds from Saint Vey's keepers
- secure a written seal or authority from Voss, forged or real
- build route traps or anchor lines on the broken watchroad
- gain boat access and local guidance from Nera Vale
- confront or protect Old Sella to alter Mirefold support

The goal is to make preparation reduce risk, open routes, or reshape the final moral choice.

### Phase 5: Confrontation at the Flood Chamber

The player reaches a collapsed sluice zone beneath the Sunk Watchroad during high water.

There they find:

- split grain barges snagged in reed channels
- exposed old stonework with Compact seals
- disturbed dead fused with marsh debris and ritual offerings
- living smugglers or guards trying to salvage grain before witnesses arrive

The confrontation should combine:

- hostile humans protecting the cover-up
- an emergent dead-marsh entity or cluster phenomenon
- environmental danger from water surges, mud collapse, and low visibility

## Signature Hostile Force

Use a region-specific threat called the Reedbound.

Concept:
Not a single elegant monster, but a massed dead-water emergence made of disturbed corpses, root fibers, drowned grain sacks, reeds, and old binding materials from the flood chamber.

Why it works:

- visually unique to The Hazemoor
- tied to buried history instead of generic undead
- supports both combat and ritual containment logic
- makes the marsh feel like an active witness to atrocity

## Doctrine-Specific Strengths

### Judicator

Strong options:

- force testimony conflicts into confession
- use lawful authority to seize records or compel escorts
- pass sentence publicly on the responsible official or village conspirator

Payoff style:
Restores visible order quickly, but may preserve the larger system.

### Stalker

Strong options:

- reconstruct the altered route early
- detect false tracks and night traffic patterns
- prepare the terrain to isolate both smugglers and the Reedbound

Payoff style:
Finds the real sequence of events most efficiently and turns the final confrontation into a hunt.

### Lantern

Strong options:

- interpret the shrine offerings correctly as partial containment
- ward against the Reedbound's fear effects and corpse surge
- perform a controlled rite to quiet or redirect the dead

Payoff style:
Reveals the metaphysical truth and can reduce bloodshed, but alarms the public.

### Broker

Strong options:

- trade protection, silence, or supplies for testimony
- recruit smugglers, ferrymen, or dirty guards as temporary assets
- manipulate which faction absorbs blame and which survives politically

Payoff style:
Produces the most flexible resolution, but often leaves rot alive under the floorboards.

## Resolution Paths

The quest should support several valid outcomes.

### Outcome A: Public Exposure

The player reveals grain diversion, burial crimes, and the flood-chamber breach.

Effects:

- Тихий Шелест enters open unrest
- Voss falls or is executed
- Mirefold gains short-term relief but loses protection
- faction trust splinters
- player gains reputation for truth over stability

### Outcome B: Controlled Containment

The player suppresses the broader scandal but neutralizes the Reedbound and forces limited reform.

Effects:

- the region stabilizes temporarily
- grain distribution resumes unevenly
- the deeper crime remains officially buried
- player gains institutional trust and personal disgust

### Outcome C: Local Bargain

The player works with Sella, ferrymen, or smugglers to redirect supplies and maintain an unofficial peace.

Effects:

- Mirefold survives the season more intact
- Тихий Шелест weakens politically
- corruption deepens but becomes decentralized
- player gains practical allies and questionable reputation

Current default-canon direction: this is the preferred benchmark outcome for ongoing worldbuilding.

### Outcome D: Ritual Mercy

The player prioritizes laying the disturbed dead properly and accepts that supplies or political order may collapse in the short term.

Effects:

- the supernatural pressure recedes most cleanly
- famine risk worsens before recovery is possible
- Saint Vey's keepers gain influence
- player becomes feared as someone who chooses the dead over the living present

## Visible Consequences

After quest completion, the region should visibly change through some combination of:

- ration policy in Тихий Шелест
- civilian dialogue and rumor tone
- access to supplies or ferrymen services
- guard presence on the Sunk Watchroad
- conditions in Mirefold
- altered shrine or burial visuals near Saint Vey's

## Why This Works as a Benchmark

This quest proves the game if it can make the player feel all of the following in one chain:

- confusion becoming understanding
- preparation becoming power
- moral clarity being denied
- violence being only one form of resolution
- local consequence feeding the larger regional mystery

## Follow-Up Hooks

This quest can naturally lead into:

- investigation of other Compact drainage sites
- retaliation from fortress-city authorities
- a deeper relationship with Saint Vey's keepers
- smuggler contact chains across the fen
- evidence that the Hollow Season follows hidden infrastructure rather than random curse zones
