# Quest Brief: Salt in the Ledger

## Purpose

`Salt in the Ledger` is the first full follow-up quest after the default-canon Local Bargain outcome of `The Hunger Below`.

Its job is to prove that an unofficial survival network does not stay morally clean just because it began as a necessity.

This quest must demonstrate:

- trust becoming a contested resource
- corruption mutating through local systems, not only state systems
- a morally useful compromise beginning to generate second-order harm
- faction and reputation systems reacting to method as much as outcome

## Starting State

Assume the player has already become known in The Hazemoor as a Warden willing to work in the mud when institutions fail.

This creates a dangerous condition:
People now route requests, favors, supplies, and lies through the player's previous compromise.

## Quest Hook

A covert medicine shipment reaches Сонк-Феррі through routes protected after `The Hunger Below`, but part of the stock has been diluted with marsh water and false salts. Several sick civilians worsen after treatment. Shrine seals on the crates appear genuine at first glance, but something in the wax, numbering, or burial ash marking is wrong.

The first accusation is immediate and convenient: smugglers have turned greedier than hunger.

The truth is messier.

## Core Mystery

What happened to the medicine?

The layered truth should allow multiple partial conclusions before full resolution.

Actual structure:

- some medicine was stolen and replaced
- forged shrine marks were used to frame or protect someone
- one local intermediary believed diluted medicine was better than no medicine and tried to stretch supply
- a predatory smuggler cell recognized that the player's bargain created a trusted channel and decided to parasitize it
- signs exist that someone inside Сонк-Феррі wants the network to look irredeemable so a purge becomes politically easy

This makes the case a knot of:

- practical desperation
- opportunistic corruption
- political sabotage
- reputational warfare

## Main Cast

### Brin Oss

Soft-spoken logistics broker.

Role:
Claims to want the network cleaned before it collapses, but never offers information for free.

Use:
Can be ally, manipulator, or controlled leak depending on player approach.

### Hara Pike

Predatory smuggler route captain.

Role:
Treats suffering as stable demand and sees the Warden's previous compromise as the opening of a market.

Use:
Embodies how useful gray systems become profitable rot.

### Brother Caros

Shrine keeper of Saint Vey.

Role:
Knows the forged seals are close enough to implicate someone trained around burial handling, and fears a scandal that will ruin real aid channels.

Use:
Connects shrine credibility to material survival.

### Tovan Reed

Clan spokesman.

Role:
Believes the city will use any mistake as justification for direct control, so he pressures the player to solve quietly.

Use:
Embodies local fear of losing fragile autonomy.

### Mara Dens

Makeshift healer in Сонк-Феррі.

Role:
Administered the medicine and noticed too late that some stock was wrong. She may have hidden evidence to avoid panic.

Use:
Provides civilian pressure and emotional weight.

## Quest Structure

### Phase 1: Immediate Harm

The player enters Сонк-Феррі after several patients worsen under treatment.

Opening conditions:

- families accuse smugglers, shrine keepers, or ferrymen depending on who they already hate
- an opened crate bears seal marks linked to Saint Vey's
- Mara Dens begs the player not to let the crowd destroy the remaining stock before it can be examined

Goal:
Force the player into a crisis where truth and panic compete in real time.

### Phase 2: Forensic Inquiry

The player investigates the shipment's chain of custody.

Evidence lanes:

- inspect wax, twine, numbering cuts, and ash signatures on crate seals
- compare medicine smell, texture, sediment, and effect on the sick
- trace ferry logs, hidden handoff points, and who handled the stock last
- examine whether the tampering happened at origin, during transit, or after arrival
- pressure Brin Oss, Tovan Reed, or shrine assistants for conflicting testimony

What this phase should reveal:

- the forgery is skilled but not perfect
- the tampering likely happened in more than one step
- at least one person acted from panic, not greed
- someone wants all blame to land in one obvious direction

### Phase 3: Pressure and Interpretation

The player must decide which theory to privilege while the situation worsens.

Possible emerging theories:

- Hara Pike's network is exploiting the trust route for profit
- locals stretched supply out of desperation and created a fatal lie
- shrine-linked handling was copied by someone close to burial practice
- administrative agents are sharpening a case for crackdown by feeding the scandal

This phase should put the player under time pressure so interpretation is not leisurely.

### Phase 4: Preparation

Preparation here is more social and procedural than purely martial.

Available preparations:

- secure Brother Caros's cooperation to verify true versus false seals
- set a false follow-up shipment to bait the tampering point
- station clan lookouts or ferrymen at transfer routes
- isolate remaining medicine stock and create safe triage rules
- obtain leverage over Brin Oss to force a controlled meeting

The player should feel they are building a case trap, not just gearing for combat.

### Phase 5: Confrontation

The final confrontation should occur at a hidden transfer site where medicine, forged seals, and private ledgers overlap.

Recommended location:
A salt-drying shed on rotting pilings near a half-collapsed ferry cut, used as a staging point for disguised aid shipments.

What the player finds:

- false stock being prepared or redistributed
- ledger fragments tying medicine flow to both unofficial routes and official shortages
- proof that Hara Pike's people are involved, but not alone
- evidence that one local actor tried to stretch supply and created the opening for larger abuse

The confrontation may include:

- armed smugglers or hired route-guards
- a civilian intermediary who panics and runs
- a fire or water hazard that threatens the remaining real medicine

## Doctrine-Specific Strengths

### Judicator

Strong options:

- build a clean chain-of-custody case
- force testimony under procedural threat
- turn the final confrontation into a public legal collapse for the guilty

Risk:
Too much formal exposure may shatter the whole informal aid network at once.

### Stalker

Strong options:

- reconstruct hidden route transfers with high accuracy
- identify which boot patterns, boat wear, or cargo handling marks do not belong
- catch the tampering cell in motion rather than by confession

Risk:
Strong field proof may still fail to settle the political meaning of the case.

### Lantern

Strong options:

- distinguish authentic shrine handling from imitated ritual debris
- use burial residue knowledge to detect forged sacred legitimacy
- calm panic among the sick through controlled spiritual authority

Risk:
Public reliance on uncanny methods may deepen fear of the Warden's role.

### Broker

Strong options:

- fracture the alliance between opportunists and frightened locals
- buy or trade silence to identify the real parasite in the network
- preserve useful channels while cutting out only the most dangerous actors

Risk:
The player may solve the immediate problem while normalizing private coercion.

## Resolution Paths

### Outcome A: Purge the Parasites

The player exposes Hara Pike's predatory cell and allows a brutal cleanup of the route.

Effects:

- short-term trust in medicine recovers
- the network becomes narrower and more fearful
- officials use the scandal to justify heavier surveillance
- some civilians lose access because the route is now too dangerous to use

### Outcome B: Quiet Correction

The player hides the broader scandal, removes the worst actors, and rebuilds the medicine route under tighter informal rules.

Effects:

- practical survival improves
- local trust in the player deepens
- hidden coercion becomes more accepted as governance
- future blackmail risk rises

### Outcome C: Public Fracture

The player proves that the network itself has become compromised and refuses to protect its legitimacy.

Effects:

- faction trust collapses across local actors
- shrine keepers and clans blame each other openly
- the administration gains a window to intervene
- the player gains moral clarity at major social cost

### Outcome D: Controlled Scapegoat

The player sacrifices one guilty layer publicly while shielding the broader structure.

Effects:

- order returns faster than truth
- one faction gains disproportionate control over the surviving route
- resentment hardens beneath the surface
- the player is seen as useful but dangerous by everyone involved

## Reputation Consequences

Public Memory:
This quest should strongly affect whether the player is seen as a protector, fixer, liar, or necessary contaminant.

Faction Standing:
This quest should create significant shifts with smugglers, shrine keepers, Мурі, and the administration.

Professional Reputation:
Likely traits reinforced here are dependable, compromised, lawful, or dangerous depending on method.

## Why This Quest Matters

This quest is where the game proves it understands the price of gray morality. `The Hunger Below` let the player choose compromise. `Salt in the Ledger` asks whether the player understands that compromise does not stay still once other people learn to live inside it.

## Follow-Up Hooks

This quest should feed directly into:

- `The Ferry Oath`, by intensifying disputes over route control
- `The Quota Knife`, by giving the administration evidence or pretext
- deeper mistrust between mercy keepers and strict keepers at Saint Vey's
- a larger question of whether the player is building resilience or a shadow regime
