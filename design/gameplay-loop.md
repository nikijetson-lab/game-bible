# Core Gameplay Loop

## One-Sentence Loop

The player enters a troubled place, uncovers what the threat really is, prepares accordingly, and resolves the situation through combat, judgment, negotiation, or compromise, with consequences that persist in the region.

## Primary Loop

1. Arrival
The player reaches a settlement, roadside ruin, hunting ground, or contested route where something is wrong.

2. Inquiry
The player gathers testimony, rumors, tracks, physical evidence, and faction signals to understand the visible problem and the hidden one underneath it.

3. Interpretation
The player decides what they believe is actually happening. This is where uncertainty matters: a monster case may be political, a murder may be ritual, and a haunting may be a cover for resource control.

4. Preparation
The player chooses gear, consumables, knowledge, leverage, route, and allies. Preparation should create meaningful advantage, not clerical busywork.

5. Confrontation
The player resolves the case through one or more forms of pressure:

- direct combat
- hunting and pursuit
- social judgment
- intimidation or bargaining
- exposing evidence
- controlled sacrifice

6. Consequence
The outcome changes something in the local state: trust, fear, leadership, scarcity, faction influence, access, or future threats.

7. Return Pressure
The player collects payment, reputation, enemies, or new leads, which push them toward the next case and the wider regional crisis.

## Why This Loop Matters

This loop combines narrative and mechanics instead of splitting them into separate modes. Investigation informs preparation. Preparation affects combat and social options. Resolution alters the world state. The player is not just clearing icons from a map; they are processing unstable situations.

## Supporting Secondary Loops

### Travel and Discovery

While moving between cases, the player encounters:

- ambushes and opportunistic violence
- environmental storytelling
- hidden shrines, graves, ruins, or watchposts
- contracts, rumors, and emergent local problems
- signs of the Hollow Season spreading through the land

Travel should feel tense and revealing, not like empty transit.

### Resource and Preparation Loop

The player acquires and manages:

- weapon and armor upkeep
- anti-monster tools or substances
- field remedies and survival supplies
- ritual or investigative materials
- favors, warrants, and social leverage

This loop should reward foresight while staying light enough to preserve tempo.

### Reputation and Regional State

The player builds a layered reputation:

- common people remember outcomes
- factions track alignment and usefulness
- settlements change availability and mood
- certain lies or truths stay alive depending on player choices

The goal is not a simplistic morality meter. It is a memory of how the player operates.

## Session-Level Rhythm

A good play session should usually contain:

- one compelling problem discovery
- one investigation phase with at least one surprise
- one meaningful preparation choice
- one tense resolution sequence
- one visible regional consequence or strong narrative reveal

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- investigation that becomes pixel-hunting
- preparation that feels like checklist admin
- combat that ignores what the player learned beforehand
- choices that look dramatic but do not alter state
- travel segments that dilute tension with filler distance

## Signature Pitch

This is a dark fantasy RPG where every case is a pressure cooker: you read the place, decide what truth to trust, and live with what your judgment does to the region.
