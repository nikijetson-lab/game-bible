# Quest Brief: Ash Under the Chapel

## Purpose

`Ash Under the Chapel` is the third full follow-up quest after the default-canon Local Bargain outcome of `The Hunger Below`.

Where the first two follow-ups dealt with material survival and political control, this quest shifts into spiritual territory. The player must now face that every compromise they enabled has taught the region a new language for what is thinkable—and someone is now speaking it wrong.

This quest must demonstrate:

- the metaphysical cost of practical bargains
- folk belief being weaponized without understanding
- the Shrine Keepers' internal split brought to the surface
- the player's doctrine path tested in ritual logic, not just investigation
- the region learning dangerous lessons from the Warden's own methods

## Starting State

By this point the player has:

- made the Local Bargain in `The Hunger Below`
- investigated and resolved the medicine corruption in `Salt in the Ledger`
- taken a position on route governance in `The Ferry Oath`
- become a visible authority figure in The Hazemoor
- weakened the shrine keepers' public credibility through association with unofficial channels
- shown the region that forbidden accommodations can work, at least in the short term

The region is now asking a quiet question that no one wants to say aloud:
*If the Warden can bargain with smugglers and choose survival over purity, why can't we?*

## Quest Hook

Brother Caros comes to the player privately. A corpse was found laid in a half-submerged burial niche on the edge of the fen, arranged with ash markings, reed bindings, and a broken lantern. It mimics the ritual patterns that Old Sella of Mirefold used to keep the Reedbound away during the grain convoy crisis.

But the person who did this did not know the full rite.

The body is starting to draw attention from the marsh—not because it is dead, but because it was prepared wrong. Something is answering the incomplete signal.

Brother Caros begs the player to investigate before Saint Vey's strict keepers discover the imitation and launch a doctrinal purge that would destroy the mercy keepers and expose the concession made in Mirefold.

## Core Mystery

The immediate question is who performed the imitation rite and why.

The layered truth:

- the rite was performed by a desperate villager who overheard fragments of what Mirefold did and tried to replicate it for a loved one who died badly
- the imitation worked *partially*—something did respond, but it was not peace or containment
- the corpse is now a beacon for a deeper disturbance in the fen's dead-water channels
- someone in the shrine network knows that the Mirefold accommodation was recorded and may leak it to the strict keepers as leverage
- the strict keepers have been waiting for exactly this kind of incident to prove that mercy is dangerous

This makes the case a pressure cooker of:

- sincere grief weaponized by circumstances
- doctrinal war hiding behind pastoral concern
- supernatural contamination that is real but not inevitable
- the mercy keepers' hidden archive becoming a political liability

## Main Cast

### Brother Caros (quest giver)

Field keeper of Saint Vey's, compassionate, evasive, keeper of dangerous records.

Role:
He discovered the corpse and knows the doctrinal danger. He needs the player to investigate quietly and contain the evidence if it implicates Mirefold's methods.

His arc:
Caros is the face of mercy-aligned shrine faith. This quest will determine whether he survives as a credible keeper or is broken by the doctrinal split.

### Old Sella of Mirefold

Village elder, keeper of folk-offerings, wartime accommodator of desperate rites.

Role:
She did not teach the rite to anyone, but someone learned by watching. She is devastated that her survival tactic has become a danger to others.

Her arc:
Sella must face whether her bargain was wisdom or corruption in a form she did not recognize.

### Mother Ysra Veyn

Senior custodian of Saint Vey's, willing to hide doctrine-breaking practices for the sake of reduced suffering.

Role:
She knows the mercy keepers' archive is vulnerable and may decide to destroy or weaponize it depending on how the crisis unfolds.

Her arc:
This quest forces her to choose whether to protect the keepers or protect the truth, and whether those are still the same thing.

### Skel Hanis (imitation practitioner)

A fen-drainage worker from a hamlet near the secondary burial site.

Role:
Lost his brother to the marsh-fever after the grain crisis. He saw what Mirefold did. He tried to do the same with half the knowledge and full desperation.

His arc:
Skel is not malicious. He is a mirror of what the player's compromise looks like without context, training, or institutional support.

### Deacon Rane (strict keeper opposition)

A young, zealous shrine disciplinarian who suspects that mercy keepers have been hiding forbidden practice.

Role:
He is not wrong that something dangerous is happening. He is dangerous because he is right for the wrong institutional reasons.

His arc:
Rane's investigation runs parallel to the player's, and how the player handles him defines whether the keepers fracture or reform.

### The Responding Presence (supernatural force)

Not a single entity, but a phenomenon.

Working concept:
The incomplete rite created a signal that the dead-water channels recognize but cannot interpret correctly. What responds is not a monster in the normal sense—it is *attention* from something that does not distinguish between a proper burial and a wrong one.

When the player reaches the site of the imitation burial, the following conditions may apply depending on earlier choices:

- the marsh has risen around the niche
- local wildlife avoids the area
- roots from nearby trees have grown into the body
- voices from the water repeat names from sealed burial rolls
- the corpse is not where it was laid but moves in response to proximity

## Quest Structure

### Phase 1: Quiet Discovery

The player meets Brother Caros at a hidden location away from Saint Vey's.

Opening conditions:

- Caros describes the body and its arrangement in detail
- he admits the rite's resemblance to Mirefold but insists he never documented it
- he reveals that someone has been asking about burial records at the Lantern House
- a strict keeper (Deacon Rane) has begun auditing mercy keeper activities

Goal:
Establish that this is a time-sensitive spiritual crisis, not a simple burial violation.

### Phase 2: Tracing the Imitation

The player investigates who performed the rite and what they used as sources.

Investigation lanes:

- examine the corpse and the burial niche for tool marks, source materials, and mistake patterns
- trace who accessed burial records, ash supplies, or ritual reeds in recent days
- interview Mirefold villagers about whether anyone watched Sella's work during the crisis
- speak with drainage workers to identify Skel Hanis and understand his loss
- determine whether the imitation was taught, observed, or invented

What this phase should reveal:

- the imitation was purely observational—no one taught it
- Skel Hanis genuinely believed he was helping his brother rest
- the rites were partially effective in a way that surprised everyone
- strict keepers are already collecting evidence but do not yet know the source
- something in the fen is paying attention to the wrong signal

### Phase 3: The Doctrinal Reckoning

The player must decide how to contain the spiritual and political damage before the strict keepers act.

Key pressures:

- Brother Caros wants the imitation buried quietly and the corpse given proper rites
- Mother Ysra Veyn must decide whether to protect or expose the mercy archive
- Deacon Rane grows closer to the truth with each passing day
- Skel Hanis is terrified and may confess to the wrong people
- Old Sella wants to help fix what her methods inspired

This phase is the moral heart of the quest. The player must decide not just *what happened*, but *what must be done about the fact that compromise teaches others*.

### Phase 4: Preparation

Ritual and social preparation before the final confrontation at the burial site.

Available actions:

- work with Brother Caros to reconstruct the incomplete rite into a proper closure
- negotiate with Mother Ysra Veyn to seal or transfer the mercy archive
- confront or protect Skel Hanis depending on whether he is a witness or a liability
- gain Old Sella's cooperation to calm the spiritual disturbance with full knowledge
- set terms with Deacon Rane: exposure of the imitation with limits, or full doctrinal investigation
- prepare ward materials, route markers, or escort for the burial site approach

Design note:
Preparation here should feel like *spiritual surgery*—the player is deciding which parts of the truth to expose and which to seal.

### Phase 5: Confrontation at the Submerged Chapel

The quest culminates at a half-flooded remnants of an old Compact-era chapel structure, used now as a secondary burial site for the fen's poor.

The site:

- stone walls tilting into black water
- broken saint lamps hanging from rusted chains
- burial niches cut into the foundation, some open, some sealed with peat and ash
- roots and reed overgrowth obscuring original shrine markings

What the player finds:

- the imitation corpse has shifted position since discovery
- the responding presence manifests as a localized dead-water phenomenon: wrong reflections, displaced air, hearing voices that do not match living speakers
- Skel Hanis may be present, trying to undo his mistake
- Deacon Rane may arrive to document evidence
- the phenomenon resolves differently depending on how the player has prepared

Resolution possibilities:

- proper completion of the rite with Caros and Sella quiets the disturbance
- violent suppression of the presence damages the site but contains the immediate danger
- doctrinal confrontation between Rane and the mercy keepers occurs in real time
- the archive is destroyed, sealed, or surrendered depending on player choices

## Doctrine-Specific Strengths

### Judicator

Strong options:

- establish a binding procedural line between Mirefold's wartime necessity and the imitation's recklessness
- formalize a shrine custody protocol that addresses both past accommodation and future boundaries
- rule on the doctrinal question with enough authority to silence both strict and mercy extremes

Risk:
Formal judgment may fail to heal a spiritual wound.

### Stalker

Strong options:

- trace the responding presence to its source through terrain patterns and dead-water movement
- anticipate where the phenomenon will manifest next
- capture or kill the physical source of the disturbance if it has one

Risk:
Solving the material problem may miss the spiritual question entirely.

### Lantern

Strong options:

- interpret the incomplete rite and complete it with appropriate spiritual authority
- read the doctrinal archive as a living document rather than a weapon
- calm the disturbance through ritual coherence rather than force

Risk:
Deepening the player's mystical reputation may alienate civic actors further.

### Broker

Strong options:

- negotiate a truce between strict and mercy keepers that preserves both credibility
- trade Skel Hanis's safety for information on who compiled the imitation record
- protect the mercy archive as a bargaining chip for later political use

Risk:
Every trade deepens the entanglement between spiritual truth and political utility.

## Resolution Paths

### Outcome A: Merciful Containment

The player works with Brother Caros and Old Sella to complete the rite properly, quiet the disturbance, and protect Skel Hanis from punishment.

Effects:

- the immediate supernatural threat recedes
- strict keepers suspect but do not prove mercy keeper wrongdoing
- the mercy archive remains intact but more carefully hidden
- the player gains deep trust with mercy-aligned shrine keepers
- a lesson is learned quietly: incomplete imitation is dangerous, but the impulse was human

### Outcome B: Doctrinal Purge

The player allows or assists Deacon Rane in exposing the mercy keepers' accommodation and purging forbidden practice from Saint Vey's.

Effects:

- shrine practice is purified quickly and publicly
- strict keepers consolidate control
- Brother Caros is disgraced or expelled
- Old Sella and Mirefold become symbols of corruption
- the fen loses a source of compassionate care and spiritual flexibility
- the player gains reputation with strict institutional power and loses credibility with locals

### Outcome C: Controlled Revelation

The player negotiates a middle path: the imitation is acknowledged, the mercy archive is partially surrendered or reformed, and Deacon Rane is given enough truth to justify restraint.

Effects:

- both strict and mercy keepers survive with reduced credibility
- a reformed doctrine is created that binds future accommodation
- Shrine authority weakens but becomes more honest
- the player is seen as a mediator of spiritual crisis rather than a partisan
- both sides have grievances, but violence is avoided

### Outcome D: Buried Truth

The player destroys the body, seals the site, and suppresses all evidence linking the imitation to Mirefold or the mercy keepers.

Effects:

- the spiritual disturbance is contained physically
- the doctrinal crisis is deferred
- Brother Caros survives but lives with guilt
- the mercy archive remains hidden and usable
- the player gains control over the narrative at the cost of being the only one who knows the full truth
- the region learns nothing from the incident except that secrecy works

## Reputation Consequences

Public Memory:
This quest redefines what the fen believes about the player's relationship to faith, the dead, and the shrine keepers. The player becomes seen as either a spiritual ally, a necessary enforcer, a dangerous mystic, or someone who uses the sacred as a management tool.

Faction Standing:
This quest creates the most significant shift so far with the Shrine Keepers. It also sends a signal to all other factions about whether the player respects institutional boundaries.

Professional Reputation:
Likely trait reinforcement: uncanny, lawful, compromised, or dependable.

## Why This Quest Matters

This quest is where the game confirms that the player is not just a political operative with a badge. The compromise chain that began with `The Hunger Below` is now recognized spiritually. People are learning from the Warden's example in ways the Warden never intended.

`Ash Under the Chapel` forces a reckoning that material and political quests could not reach: *what does it mean when your methods become someone else's faith?*

## Follow-Up Hooks

This quest should feed directly into:

- `The Quota Knife`, as shrine instability makes the administration's move more urgent
- deeper access to Shrine Keeper archives for players who protected mercy keepers
- a long-term doctrinal thread that may affect other regions' shrine networks
- tension between mercy keepers and strict keepers that may erupt later
- evidence that the incomplete rite attracted attention from something older than the Hollow Season
