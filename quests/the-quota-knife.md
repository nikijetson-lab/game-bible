# Quest Brief: The Quota Knife

## Purpose

`The Quota Knife` is the fourth and culminating quest of the The Hazemoor follow-up chain after the default-canon Local Bargain outcome of `The Hunger Below`.

Every previous quest in this chain asked a different question about compromise:

- *Salt in the Ledger*: does the network stay clean just because it started as necessity?
- *The Ferry Oath*: who should control the infrastructure of survival?
- *Ash Under the Chapel*: what does it mean when your methods become someone else's faith?

This quest asks the final question:
*Were you solving a crisis, or were you founding a shadow government?*

The administration has stopped watching from the margins. It is now sharpening a blade designed to cut through every informal structure the player helped create.

This quest must demonstrate:

- the full consequence chain of the player's choices across all prior quests
- the Fortress-City Administration as a serious pressure system, not a distant noise
- the player forced to choose between the order they rejected and the disorder they enabled
- every faction relationship built (or burned) in the previous three quests becoming active leverage
- the region's understanding of the player as something more permanent than a passing Warden

## Starting State

By this point, The Hazemoor has been shaped by the player's decisions across four quests:

- **Survival network**: active, compromised, partially controlled by trusted or predatory actors
- **Route governance**: either consolidated under Tovan, fragmented under Nera, player-imposed, or seized by the city
- **Shrine integrity**: either preserved, purged, reformed, or suppressed
- **Player reputation**: known as a protector, fixer, conspirator, or spiritual actor—but in all cases, *someone who matters*

The region is functional but unstable. Everyone with power has aligned or resented the player. The city's patience has reached its limit.

## Quest Hook

Marshal-Recorder Serit Kelm arrives in Сонк-Феррі with a sealed commission and a small armed escort. He does not come to negotiate. He comes to restore legal discipline.

His mandate:

- audit all grain and medicine distribution records since the convoy crisis
- identify and charge those who diverted state supplies
- establish formal governance over the ferry network
- document any unauthorized rites, shrine abuses, or civil disorder
- determine whether the Pale Warden operating in The Hazemoor is an asset, a liability, or a target

He requests a formal meeting with the player. It is not a threat. It is a courtesy. The courtesy means: *this is your last chance to choose which side of the record you appear on.*

## Core Conflict

The quest is not a mystery. The facts of what happened are largely known. The conflict is about *what gets written down as truth* and *who carries the cost*.

Serit Kelm is not a villain. He is a lawful actor who believes that order is fragile and that the institutions maintaining it must be protected from exposure of their own crimes. He will:

- accept a version of events that protects the administration's legitimacy
- prosecute some actors and protect others depending on political usefulness
- offer the player a way out if they help stabilize the narrative
- escalate to force if the player refuses to cooperate

The player's choice is not *good versus evil*. It is:

- accept the city's order with its buried crimes and continued control
- protect the informal structures that saved lives but are now compromised
- force a full reckoning that may collapse both systems
- carve out a third space that preserves some local autonomy within a reformed framework

## Main Cast

### Marshal-Recorder Serit Kelm

City legal officer, methodical, patient, ice-cold.

Role:
The face of institutional discipline arriving to rewrite the region's recent history into an administratively acceptable form.

His logic:
He genuinely believes that truth without order is chaos, and that chaos kills more people than lies. He is not corrupt in the way Voss was—he is principled in a way that serves the structure rather than justice.

His offer to the player:
Provide a clean account that protects the administration, identifies limited scapegoats, and establishes formal oversight. In return, the player retains operational status, avoids prosecution, and may continue working in the region under supervision.

His threat:
If the player refuses, Kelm will investigate everyone the player worked with. He will find charges that stick. He will dismantle the informal network piece by piece, and the player will be named as the architect of a local insurgency.

### Quartermaster Halren Voss (survivor, depending on prior choices)

If Voss survived the prior quest chain, he is now Kelm's primary source.

His role:
Voss knows where the grain diversion started and which official channels were compromised. He will trade that information for personal protection. He is Kelm's evidence chain against everyone except himself.

### Tovan Reed (dependent on Ferry Oath outcome)

His role:
Tovan may be an ally protecting the route council, a rival who resents the player's interference, or a broken figure if the council was seized. His testimony and cooperation significantly affect what Kelm can prove.

### Nera Vale

Her role:
Nera is not politically sophisticated enough to survive Kelm's inquiry alone. She needs protection, or she will be broken into testimony that damages the entire local network.

### Brother Caros / Mother Ysra Veyn (dependent on Ash Under the Chapel outcome)

Their role:
The shrine keepers are the administration's biggest vulnerability. If the mercy archive survived, it contains proof that the player worked with forbidden practices. If it was purged, the keepers have less to fear but also less leverage.

### Brin Oss

His role:
Oss will read the room and sell information to whoever pays best. His loyalty is to survival, not to the player. He is Kelm's easiest route into the smuggling network's connections to the player.

### Warden-Curate Ilya Marr (potential emergence)

Her role:
If the player's reputation has attracted Warden Remnant attention, Ilya Marr may appear to offer an alternative framework: a Warden-curated account that exposes Compact crimes without collapsing the region. This is the confessor path option.

## Quest Structure

### Phase 1: The Invitation

The player receives a formal summons to meet Serit Kelm at the Сонк-Феррі ration office, now converted into his field headquarters.

Opening conditions:

- the office is guarded, organized, and filing records visibly
- Kelm offers tea, patience, and a clear legal summary of what he knows
- he presents a written account of the convoy crisis, the medicine corruption, the ferry dispute, and the shrine incident
- the account is *mostly accurate* but assigns blame to local actors and protects administrative figures
- he asks the player to sign it as a witness, not a defendant

Goal:
Establish that Kelm knows everything but is offering a controlled settlement, not a purge—yet.

### Phase 2: The Audit

The player has limited time before Kelm's investigation progresses to arrests.

During this phase the player can:

- warn allies to hide evidence, flee, or prepare testimony
- gather counter-leverage: proof of administrative corruption that Kelm cannot bury
- secure promises of silence or testimony from Tovan, Nera, Caros, Oss, or others
- destroy or protect records from the mercy archive, ferry logs, or smuggling ledgers
- decide whether to contact Warden remnants for institutional support

Design note:
This phase should feel like a race to build a position before Kelm locks the frame. The player cannot save everyone. They must choose who is protected and who is sacrificed.

### Phase 3: The Testimonies

Kelm summons key actors for formal recorded testimony. The player can attend, influence, or intervene in each session.

Key testimony sessions:

- Halren Voss: will testify against everyone except himself unless the player has evidence that ties him to the original diversion
- Nera Vale: may break under questioning unless the player has prepared her or provided representation
- Tovan Reed: will protect his own position first, the network second, and the player third
- Brother Caros: may choose to shield or expose shrine secrets depending on the spiritual outcome
- Brin Oss: will volunteer damaging truths unless the player has purchased his silence or redirected his testimony

The player's interventions can change the record, but Kelm will notice each intervention and adjust his final judgment accordingly.

### Phase 4: Preparation for the Reckoning

After testimony, Kelm will issue his findings and recommendations to the city. The player has one last window to shape the outcome.

Available actions:

- assemble a counter-report with evidence of administrative crimes
- negotiate a settlement with Kelm that sacrifices specific actors to protect the network's core
- prepare local actors for resistance if the player plans to refuse the settlement
- seek external authority: a Warden Curate's statement, a shrine custodian's religious exemption, a smuggler's evidence of state corruption
- mobilize public sentiment in Сонк-Феррі to make punitive action politically costly

### Phase 5: The Reckoning

The quest culminates in a formal reading at the Сонк-Феррі ration office with witnesses, guards, and the key actors present.

Kelm reads his findings and announces consequences.

The scene should feel like a tribunal where the verdict was drafted before the hearing began, but the player's choices will determine what actually happens.

The confrontation includes:

- Kelm's announced charges and assignments of responsibility
- the player's opportunity to accept, challenge, or refuse the findings
- immediate reactions from each present faction member
- potential violence, flight, or public disruption depending on how the settlement is received
- a closing statement from Kelm that frames the region's future

## Doctrine-Specific Strengths

### Judicator

Strong options:

- match Kelm in procedural language and force a formal evidentiary challenge
- construct a counter-case that Kelm cannot dismiss without violating his own legal frame
- demand a formal Warden jurisdictional review that buys time and authority

Risk:
The player becomes what they are challenging—a creature of procedure.

### Stalker

Strong options:

- discover evidence Kelm's investigators missed
- track the real movement of records and grain that official accounts suppress
- use field knowledge to prove where the administration's own corruption predates the player's involvement

Risk:
Material proof may not translate into political victory.

### Lantern

Strong options:

- invoke shrine authority and burial truth to challenge the administration's moral legitimacy
- present the mercy archive as evidence that the state's own crimes created the conditions for local accommodation
- reframe the entire chain of events as spiritual consequence, not political failure

Risk:
The administration will not accept metaphysical arguments as legal defense.

### Broker

Strong options:

- negotiate the settlement that saves the most people at the cost of permanent entanglement with the administration
- trade evidence against one faction for protection of another
- secure the network's survival by accepting formal oversight with informal control underneath

Risk:
Everyone walks away alive, but the player becomes the administration's local manager of compromised peace.

## Resolution Paths

### Outcome A: Accommodation

The player signs Kelm's account, accepts limited scapegoats, and submits to formal oversight.

Effects:

- the administration retains control of the region
- some local actors are prosecuted or removed
- the remaining network survives under supervision
- the player is authorized to continue operating with restrictions
- the deeper truth of Compact crimes remains buried
- the region stabilizes at the cost of institutional legitimacy

### Outcome B: Partial Resistance

The player challenges Kelm's findings, presents counter-evidence, and negotiates a modified settlement that protects key local actors.

Effects:

- some local autonomy is preserved
- the administration is forced to acknowledge limited corruption
- Kelm returns to the city with a mixed report
- some actors are sacrificed to protect others
- the player's relationship with the administration becomes hostile but contained
- the region enters an uneasy truce between local and state power

### Outcome C: Full Reckoning

The player refuses all settlement, presents comprehensive evidence of administrative crimes, and forces a crisis that Kelm cannot contain.

Effects:

- the administration's legitimacy in The Hazemoor collapses
- local networks are exposed to full scrutiny
- some actors flee, are arrested, or face reprisal
- the player becomes a fugitive or open opponent of the fortress-city
- Warden remnants (confessor-aligned) become active allies
- the region enters an unstable period of contested authority
- the Compact burial crimes become public knowledge in the region

### Outcome D: Co-opted Control

The player negotiates a deal that preserves the network's core while giving the administration enough accountability to claim victory.

Effects:

- the player becomes the administration's recognized local authority
- informal networks continue operating under nominal oversight
- Brin Oss and survival smugglers integrate into managed supply channels
- shrine keepers operate under reformed doctrine with state tolerance
- the ferry network is jointly administered
- the player's role changes from independent Warden to frontier administrator
- long-term corruption risk persists but short-term stability is high

## Reputation Consequences

Public Memory:
This quest will be the defining memory of the player in The Hazemoor. The region will remember the player as a savior, a tool of the city, a destabilizing force, or a new local master.

Faction Standing:
Every faction's relationship with the player will settle into its final The Hazemoor configuration after this quest. Some doors close permanently. Some alliances become durable.

Professional Reputation:
This quest will reinforce whichever traits the player has consistently demonstrated, and may add a defining capstone trait: protector, administrator, fugitive, or power broker.

## Why This Quest Ends the Chain

Every previous quest in this chain introduced a new consequence of the original compromise:

- *The Hunger Below*: permission to survive
- *Salt in the Ledger*: corruption of the survival network
- *The Ferry Oath*: consolidation of the network into power
- *Ash Under the Chapel*: spiritual reflection of the network in local belief

*The Quota Knife* does not introduce a new consequence. It forces judgment on all of them at once. The administration is not reacting to one incident. It is reacting to the shape of the region the player has created.

## Follow-Up Hooks

This quest should feed into:

- the next region, where the player's The Hazemoor reputation precedes them
- lasting relationships with Warden remnants, smuggler chains, or shrine keepers based on how the reckoning was resolved
- a long-term antagonism or alliance with the Fortress-City Administration
- the player's identity crystallizing into one of the three chain directions: reluctant local protector, disciplined frontier arbiter, or emerging independent power broker
- evidence that the Hollow Season follows administrative cover-ups as closely as it follows buried dead
