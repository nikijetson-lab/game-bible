# The Hazemoor Follow-Up Chain

## Chain Goal

This document defines the next quest pressure after the default-canon `Outcome C` resolution of `The Hunger Below`.

The aim is to show how compromise creates survival, dependency, and political escalation at the same time.

## Default Starting State

Assume the player resolved `The Hunger Below` through Local Bargain.

State summary:

- Mirefold is alive, but partially dependent on unofficial supply channels
- Сонк-Феррі authority is weaker but still present
- smuggler routes are bolder and more embedded in local survival
- Мурі trust the player more than the administration does
- the administration suspects the player has become a frontier variable instead of a controllable asset

## Quest Chain Themes

The follow-up chain should push on:

- the cost of compromise
- dependency versus dignity
- who gets to mediate survival in a collapsing region
- whether unofficial systems become community resilience or a new tyranny
- whether the Warden can use dirty tools without becoming one more local power broker

## Quest 1: Salt in the Ledger

Hook:
A shipment of medicine reaches Сонк-Феррі through unofficial channels, but someone has swapped part of it with diluted stock and forged shrine seals.

Pressure:
The smuggling network that kept people alive is now being exploited by someone who understands that trust has become currency.

Main conflict:
The player must determine whether the sabotage comes from predatory smugglers, desperate locals, or administrative provocation designed to justify a crackdown.

Function:
This proves that survival networks are not automatically virtuous.

## Quest 2: The Ferry Oath

Hook:
Tovan Reed and Nera Vale clash over whether the fen's water routes should remain locally controlled, taxed secretly, or formalized under a negotiated truce.

Pressure:
The player is forced to choose whether local power should consolidate under trusted hands or remain diffuse and unstable.

Main conflict:
A key ferry crossing becomes both lifeline and choke point, drawing in smugglers, clan kin, and city watchers.

Function:
This tests whether the player wants pragmatic local order or unstable communal freedom.

## Quest 3: Ash Under the Chapel

Hook:
Brother Caros discovers evidence that someone has begun imitating Mirefold's compromise logic at another burial site without understanding the rites.

Pressure:
The bargain model is spreading beyond its original context and becoming spiritually dangerous.

Main conflict:
The player must decide whether to bury the imitation, expose it, or formalize a controlled version of it through Saint Vey's.

Function:
This pushes the idea that every compromise teaches the region what is now thinkable.

## Quest 4: The Quota Knife

Hook:
Marshal-Recorder Serit Kelm arrives with authority to restore legal discipline and identify who diverted grain, who profited, and who now governs the fen in practice.

Pressure:
The administration stops watching from afar and begins converting suspicion into structure.

Main conflict:
The player is offered a path back into official favor if they help break the unofficial order they helped create.

Function:
This is the political reckoning quest. It forces the player to answer whether they were solving a crisis or founding a shadow system.

## Chain Outcome Direction

By the end of this chain, the player should have to clarify one of three larger identities:

- reluctant local protector
- disciplined but compromised frontier arbiter
- emerging independent power broker

## Design Rule

Each follow-up quest should remember the player's doctrine path, but the larger tension stays the same:

You saved people through compromise. Now the region must live inside what that compromise taught everyone.
